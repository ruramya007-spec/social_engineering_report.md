# Social Engineering Attacks Report

## 1. Introduction

Social engineering is a type of cyber attack that targets human behavior instead of technical systems. Attackers manipulate people into revealing confidential information such as passwords, OTPs, or bank details. These attacks are very effective because they exploit trust and human psychology rather than software vulnerabilities.

---

## 2. Types of Social Engineering Attacks

### 2.1 Phishing

Phishing is one of the most common social engineering attacks.

**How it works:**
Attackers send fake emails, messages, or websites that look like they are from trusted organizations such as banks or companies.

**Example:**
A fake email asking you to “update your bank password” with a link.

**Impact:**

* Theft of login credentials
* Financial loss
* Identity theft

**Prevention:**

* Do not click unknown links
* Check email sender carefully
* Use spam filters
* Enable two-factor authentication

---

### 2.2 Pretexting

Pretexting is when an attacker creates a fake story or identity to gain trust.

**How it works:**
The attacker pretends to be someone important (like a bank officer, HR, or IT support) and asks for sensitive information.

**Example:**
A caller pretending to be from a bank asking for OTP.

**Impact:**

* Unauthorized access
* Data leakage
* Fraud

**Prevention:**

* Verify identity before sharing information
* Do not share OTP or passwords
* Be cautious with unknown calls

---

### 2.3 Baiting

Baiting involves offering something attractive to trick victims.

**How it works:**
Attackers leave infected USB drives or fake download links with tempting offers.

**Example:**
A free movie download link that installs malware.

**Impact:**

* Malware infection
* Data theft
* System damage

**Prevention:**

* Avoid unknown USB devices
* Do not download from untrusted sources
* Use antivirus software

---

## 3. Case Studies

* **Google Phishing Attack (2017):** Attackers sent fake Google Docs links that looked real. Many users clicked and gave access to their accounts.
* **Banking SMS Scams:** Users receive fake SMS messages asking them to click links and enter banking details.
* **Company Data Breach:** Employees tricked into sharing login credentials, leading to data leaks.

---

## 4. Impact on Organizations

Social engineering attacks can cause serious damage:

* Financial loss
* Loss of sensitive data
* Damage to company reputation
* Legal issues
* Loss of customer trust

---

## 5. Prevention Measures

Organizations and individuals can prevent social engineering attacks by:

* Conducting security awareness training
* Using strong passwords
* Enabling multi-factor authentication
* Verifying requests before acting
* Avoiding suspicious emails and links
* Keeping systems updated

---

## 6. Conclusion

Social engineering attacks are dangerous because they target human weaknesses. Attacks like phishing, pretexting, and baiting can lead to serious consequences such as data theft and financial loss. Awareness and proper security practices are the best ways to prevent these attacks.
